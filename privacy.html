The company Swantzter located in Sweden operates RopeScore's web services. This page was last updated 2021-02-28.

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data being processed
  • Have your personal data erased
  • Have your personal data rectified
  • Request the processing to be restricted
  • Receive data in a commonly used format
  • Lodge a complaint with a Data Protection Authority

When using RopeScore and its online services your data may be transferred and/or processed by organisations outside of the European Union, when such processing occurs Swantzter has put appropriate safeguards in place to guarantee your rights and freedoms, primarily through standard contractual clauses.

Swantzter will yearly review the data we process and remove any data for which the purpose it was collected for is no longer relevant.

For any inquiries about how Swantzter processes your data, please email privacy@swantzter.se

You can expand the sections below to learn what data Swantzter collects and how that data is used in different RopeScore services.

ropescore.com

When visiting roepscore.com Cloudflare analytics data is collected (this data is anonymized). This is used to improve our website and the delivery of our content. You can read more in Cloudflare's privacy policy. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.

ropescore.app

When visiting ropescore.app we may automatically collect errors that occurs during your usage of the app, this error collection contains some technical data about your device such as IP-address, operating system and browser version. We use this data to be able to provide a functional service. You can read more in Sentry's Data Processing Addendum. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.

If you give your explicit consent we will collect aggregated usage statistics through Google Analytics. This is used to improve our website. You can read more in Google Analytics' Terms of Service. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.

Cookies

Given your explicit consent Google Analytics may store cookies when using ropescore.app to collect statistical data. You can change your consent at any time at the start screen of ropescore.app.

RopeScore core system

By default RopeScore stores all personal data about judges and participants on your computer only.

When using RopeScore we may automatically collect errors that occurs during your usage of the program, this error collection contains some technical data about your device such as IP-address, operating system and browser version, and in rare occasions personal information such as names and club affiliation that you have stored in the app. We use this data to be able to provide a functional service. You can read more in Sentry's Data Processing Addendum. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.

If yu decide to share an export from the system with Swantzter you may be sharing names and club affiliations of participants and judges, if you do so the data will be used for troubleshooting and stored in Google Workspace. You can read more in Google Cloud's Contracts and Terms. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.

RopeScore Electronic Judging Component

When using RopeScore's add-on for electronic judging personal information such as names and club affiliations of participants and judges will be processed by Swantzter. This processing will happen in Google Cloud, region europe-west3 (Frankfurt). You can read more in Google Cloud's Contracts and Terms. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses. This processing is always based on the legal basis of contract.

When using RopeScore's add-on for electronic judging we may automatically collect errors that occurs during your usage of the program, this error collection contains some technical data about your device such as IP-address, operating system and browser version, and in rare occasions personal information such as names and club affiliation that you have stored in the app. We use this data to be able to provide a functional service. You can read more in Sentry's Data Processing Addendum. This transfer outside the EU uses standard contractual clauses.
